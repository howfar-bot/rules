How Far? — Game Rules

Last updated: March 5, 2026

1. What is How Far?

How Far? measures your living network of connections around the world. Each connection is a real distance in kilometers between two people's cities. The more connections you have and the farther apart your cities are, the greater your reach.

The leaderboard shows not "who once accumulated the most km" but who is maintaining the strongest network right now.

2. Getting Started

Path 1 — Via a friend's link. A friend sends you their link. You open @howfa_bot, share your location, the bot identifies your city, and you're offered to connect with the person who invited you. Both of you earn km.

Path 2 — Directly. You open @howfa_bot yourself, share your location, the bot identifies your city, and you get your personal connection link. You have 0 connections — grow your network by sharing your link or accepting others'.

Your city is determined once and can be changed no more than once every 90 days.

3. Connections

What is a connection?

A connection is a confirmed link between two people. When a connection is made, both earn kilometers — the distance between their cities.

How to connect

Way 1: Invite a new person. Share your link. A friend opens it, registers, accepts the connection. Both earn km.

Way 2: Connect with someone already in How Far?. Share your link with an existing user. They click it and accept. Both earn km. You don't have to bring new people — you can grow your network within the existing community.

Connection cost (points)

Each connection costs points for both sides:

  • Initiator (who shared the link): 10 points
  • Acceptor (who clicked and accepted): 5 points

If either side doesn't have enough points, the connection cannot be made.

Connection rules

  • You can't connect with yourself.
  • Only one connection between two people.
  • Same-city connections earn 10 km (minimum distance).
  • Connections are permanent — km are credited to both sides instantly.
  • Individual connections can't be broken. To delete all data — use /delete_data (removes ALL connections, km, achievements).

4. Connection Levels (L1 / L2)

L1 — Direct connections

People you connected with directly. Each L1 connection gives you km = the distance between your cities.

L2 — Connections of connections

When your L1 contact makes a new connection with someone, you earn L2 kilometers = the distance of their connection. This is the "network effect": the more active your friends are, the more your reach grows.

L2 km are credited automatically — no action needed from you.

Health affects L2

L2 km depend on your L1 friends' health. If a friend is active (100% health) — you get 100% of their L2 contribution. If a friend is inactive (50% health) — you get 50%. If fully inactive (0% health) — their L2 contribution is 0.

Your network is truly strong when your friends are active too.

5. Points

Daily allocation

  • First day: 100 points (newcomer bonus)
  • From day 2: 30 points

Unused points don't accumulate — your balance resets to the daily amount each day at 00:00 UTC.

Channel subscription bonus

Subscribing to the community channel gives +10 points/day (40 instead of 30). The bonus is active while subscribed. Available if a channel exists for your country.

6. Living Network — The Key Mechanic

What is it?

How Far? measures a living network, not static numbers. Your active score (active km) depends on how regularly you expand your network.

How it works

  • Every user has a Network Health (0-100%) indicator.
  • Health = 100% at the moment of your last connection (whether you invited or were invited).
  • If you don't make new connections, health starts to decline: -10% every 3 days without a new connection.
  • After 30 days without connections: health = 0%.
  • Any new connection = instant restoration to 100%.
  • Action = making a connection. Just opening the bot or viewing stats doesn't count.

What health affects

  • Your L1 km in the leaderboard: active_L1 = L1_km × (your health / 100)
  • Your L2 km in the leaderboard: depends on friends' health
  • Leaderboard total: active_km = active_L1 + active_L2
  • Lifetime km (total counter in /stats) — NOT affected. That's your history.
  • Global counter — NOT affected by individual health.

Grace period for newcomers

The first 7 days after registration — health doesn't decline. Time to explore the bot, invite first friends, learn the mechanics.

Coming back

Even if your health is 0% and you haven't logged in for months — one new connection instantly restores health to 100%. We're always glad to see you back. No penalties for absence.

Philosophy

Living Network is not a punishment for inactivity. It reflects reality: a real network is one you maintain and grow. The leaderboard shows who is building the strongest network in the world right now.

7. Leaderboard

The leaderboard is sorted by active_km (active_L1 + active_L2).

Public leaderboards don't reveal usernames or personal data. They show top cities and active user counts by country.

In /stats you see your rank in your city, country, and the world.

The leaderboard updates every 6 hours.

8. Achievements

Achievements are counted by lifetime km — earned achievements are never taken away when health drops. Cities and countries from both L1 and L2 connections count toward geographic achievements.

In /stats, only the highest achievement per category is shown. In /achievements, all are visible with progress.

9. Fair Play

How Far? is a game about real connections between real people. The mechanics are designed so that cheating is inherently disadvantageous:

  • Fake accounts lose health without new connections: below 50% in 2 weeks, 0% in a month. Km from connections with them fade proportionally.
  • Boosting gives a short spike, but without sustained effort the score returns to zero.
  • Real connections with real people are the only way to maintain a high active km.

What counts as a violation

  • Using automated tools (bots, scripts) for mass account and connection creation.
  • Spoofing geolocation to gain an unfair advantage.

Automatic measures

The system automatically monitors for suspicious activity. When anomalies are detected, the user receives a notification explaining the reason and duration:

  • Level 1 (warning): Temporary reduction of daily points for 48 hours. Clears automatically.
  • Level 2 (restriction): Hidden from leaderboard + reduced points. Stats and connections preserved. Clears automatically when activity normalizes.
  • Level 3 (block): Full account block. Applied only for systematic violations.
All measures are automatic — no manual intervention. Escalation and de-escalation follow the algorithm.

10. Changing Your City

  • City can be changed once every 90 days.
  • Existing connections keep their original km (distance calculated at the time of connection).
  • New connections after the change are calculated from the new city.

11. Global Counter

The main screen shows a global counter: total kilometers, people, and countries of all How Far? users. This is the collective progress of the entire community. The global counter is NOT affected by individual health.

12. Data and Privacy

  • The bot stores only your city (not exact GPS coordinates).
  • Usernames are not revealed in public leaderboards.
  • View your data: /my_data
  • Delete all your data: /delete_data
  • Inactive accounts (365 days) are cleaned up automatically.
